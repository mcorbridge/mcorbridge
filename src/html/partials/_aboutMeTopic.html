Is there a preference for the size team you work with?
I have worked on teams as small as one (me) up to 100 people. I prefer to work with smaller teams (< 20), but my experience has shown me that it is the management of the team rather than the size of the team that is most important.

Then how do you quantify good team management?
It starts out with good planning. There must be clearly defined requirements, and documents that provide a record of decisions. Project management then brings in subject matter experts in the various disciplines to provide guidance on the type of technologies to use, technical hurdles, and time estimates to solve those hurdles. Using Agile methodologies, management will then monitor progress and react to time estimates by adjusting resources or expectations. Nothing ever goes as planned. Resources (people) may be unexpectantly reallocated, unexpected technological challenges will arise, or estimates (true to their name) were not accurate enough to ensure delivery quanlity or quantity. This is the norm.

If nothing goes as planned, how do you intend to deliver good product?
This is where my decades of experience comes to play. And not just in software development. Having had the experience of being on teams that delivered product either must later than expected, or missing features that were part of requirements, it is of paramount importance that the management team 'bake' the reality of application development. All software projects are subject to three fatal mechanisms:
  1. Estimates are made by non-programmers
  2. Project Managers are unable to prevent 'scope creep'
  3. Project Managers are too dependent on estimates provided by overly confident, or overly optimistic developers
Thus from my years of experience being on both successful and unsuccessful projects, I am able to implement all my experience to ensure the highest probability of success.

What are the most valuable skills you bring to the table?
I bring a wealth of knowledge as related to designing, building and managing software development projects. I have seen projects succeed and fail - and I understand the procedures and attitudes that lead projects to success. I have experience in all facets of web technologies spanning graphics to databases - but I choose to focus my talents in front-end technologies, especially mobile Android technology, and web technology that utilizes modern Single Page App (SPA) approaches, in particular AngularJS frameworks.

Last Question. Full-time or contract positions?
I am open to both. Truthfully, it depends on the type of project, my interest in the project, the technologies involved, the people involved, location, and management. As much as possible I want to ensure success of the project by knowing that I bring the required expertise to the table, that I have support of a good team and management, and that I will continue to apply and enhance my skill set.
